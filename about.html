About the platform

Ground Engineering Toolbox is a web-based platform under development for practical, research-informed engineering tools in ground engineering.

The current prototype focuses on two areas:

  • Geothermal systems — preliminary tools for borehole heat exchanger field sizing and related thermal assessments.
  • Offshore anchor systems — preliminary tools for pile anchor response, including p–y curve generation and lateral resistance screening.

Purpose

The platform is intended to support early-stage concept development, engineering screening, teaching, and research communication. It aims to provide transparent, easy-to-use tools that make engineering assumptions and calculation logic visible to the user.

Current development stage

This website is currently in a prototype stage. The tools available at present are simplified, screening-level implementations and will continue to evolve as additional functions, validation, and engineering case studies are added.

Scope

  • Screening-level engineering calculations
  • Transparent assumptions and indicative layouts
  • Research-led tool development
  • Future expansion to additional ground engineering applications

Important note

These tools are not intended to replace detailed design, project-specific engineering judgement, code compliance checks, site investigation, or specialist consultancy input.
